# Kalshi: NBA preseason premature resolutions

## Bottom line

**Finding: This appears to be a real but temporary Kalshi operational settlement error. Confidence: medium, because the public record is thin and rests mainly on one contemporaneous report.**

Event Horizon reported that Kalshi resolved two NBA preseason game markets before the games had ended and initially showed the wrong winners. The games were **Boston vs. Toronto** and **Orlando vs. Philadelphia**, both played on Saturday, October 11, 2025 according to the report.[^event-horizon]

Kalshi then moved the affected markets back to "to be determined," and a Kalshi staffer reportedly said the issue was being reviewed and that there would be "no losses" on the outcomes.[^event-horizon]

This should be tracked as a **temporary operational-resolution failure**. The available evidence does not show a final wrong payout.

## Market details

- **Venue:** Kalshi
- **Market type:** NBA preseason game winners

## What happened

Event Horizon reported on October 11, 2025 that Kalshi had issues with two NBA preseason markets. According to the report, Kalshi resolved the markets before the games ended and determined the wrong team had won.[^event-horizon]

The report states that, by the time of publication, Kalshi's pages showed the outcomes as "to be determined" even though final scores were listed on both games.[^event-horizon]

It also reports that users complained on Kalshi's Discord and site that bets had been incorrectly graded as losses. A Kalshi staffer on Discord reportedly said the issue was being investigated and that there would be no losses on the outcomes.[^event-horizon]

Kalshi had not immediately returned Event Horizon's request for comment at the time of publication.[^event-horizon]

## The allegation against Kalshi

### 1. The markets resolved before the event was complete

If a game-winner market resolves before the game ends, the settlement system has failed at the most basic event-completion check. That is materially different from a disputed definition or a source hierarchy issue.

### 2. The initially displayed winners were wrong

Event Horizon's account says the markets were not only early, but also determined in favor of the wrong team.[^event-horizon]

The available public evidence does not identify the exact wrong side for each game in text, so that claim should be attributed to the report rather than restated as independently verified fact.

### 3. Users temporarily saw losses on markets that should not have been final

The user harm alleged in the report is temporary but real: users saw bets incorrectly graded as losses while the market should still have been unresolved.[^event-horizon]

Even if no final loss occurred, premature loss displays can affect trust, account balances, and user decisions.

## Kalshi's defense

Kalshi's defense is implicit rather than fully public.

The strongest available defense is that Kalshi did not leave the erroneous resolutions in place. Event Horizon reported that the outcomes were moved back to "to be determined" and that a Kalshi staffer said users would have no losses.[^event-horizon]

That supports a limited defense:

- the error was detected quickly;
- the markets were moved out of the wrong final state;
- users were reportedly told they would not be harmed by the erroneous determinations; and
- no final wrong payout is documented in the public source.

The defense does not eliminate the initial error. It just changes the classification from final mispayment to temporary operational failure.

## Assessment

### Did Kalshi resolve the markets incorrectly at first?

**Likely yes, based on the available report.**

Event Horizon directly reports early and wrong resolution, supported by screenshots that are embedded in the article.[^event-horizon]

### Did Kalshi later correct the state?

**Yes, according to the same report.**

The article says the markets showed as "to be determined" after the issue and that a staffer said users would have no losses.[^event-horizon]

### Is a final wrong payout established?

**No.**

The report does not show that users ultimately lost money after Kalshi reviewed the issue.

### Was this a real failure for the tracker?

**Yes, with a scope caveat.**

This is a real resolution-process failure if Event Horizon's account is accurate. But it is smaller and less conclusive than the NFL win-total case because the evidence does not show final settlement harm and no second detailed source was located.

### Overall classification

- **Operational settlement error:** Yes, temporary
- **Final mispayment:** Not established
- **Rule ambiguity:** No
- **Corrected by venue:** Reportedly yes
- **Best public verdict:** Premature/wrong market state, apparently remediated before final harm

## Precise blocker to a stronger finding

The blocker is source depth. A stronger file would need the archived Kalshi pages or API records for both markets, the final settlement records, exact wrong initial winners, and a direct Kalshi statement or support email confirming the remediation.
