# Kalshi: Trump executive orders on Day 2

## Bottom line

**Finding: This is a weak-source disputed-count allegation, not a proven Kalshi error. Confidence: low.**

The seed allegation comes from a Reddit thread about a Kalshi market asking how many executive orders Donald Trump signed on Day 2. The poster alleged that WhiteHouse.gov showed zero executive orders for January 21, 2025, while Kalshi counted one to five.[^reddit]

The same thread contains counterarguments: one commenter linked a White House page and said the rules counted repeal of an executive order as issuing an executive order.[^reddit] Another deleted/Kalshi-tagged comment said the outcome had already been explained on Kalshi Ideas and through support, but the public thread does not preserve that explanation in full.[^reddit]

This should be tracked as a blocker note unless the exact contract PDF, official Kalshi explanation, and relevant White House page are preserved.

## Market details

- **Venue:** Kalshi
- **Market:** apparently a Trump executive-order count market for January 21, 2025, described by users as "Executive Orders - Day 2."
- **Named source alleged by users:** WhiteHouse.gov.
- **Alleged result:** Kalshi counted `1-5` rather than zero.
- **Current evidence level:** Reddit-only, with disagreement inside the same thread.[^reddit]

## What happened

The Reddit poster alleged that the official source, WhiteHouse.gov, showed zero executive orders, but Kalshi claimed the outcome was 1-5.[^reddit]

Users in the same thread disputed what counted. Some argued Kalshi used an external or incorrect source, or treated a memorandum/revocation as an executive order despite the named source.[^reddit] Others argued that a qualifying White House action existed and that Kalshi's rules counted repealing an executive order.[^reddit]

The thread also includes questions directed at a Kalshi-associated account asking why the verification source did not mention outside data and why the relevant action counted as an executive order.[^reddit] It does not include a full preserved answer.

## The allegation against Kalshi

The allegation is that Kalshi counted an order that did not appear as a Day 2 executive order on the named White House source, or applied inconsistent counting rules across adjacent daily markets.

If true, that would be a source-rule failure: users bought a count tied to a named official source, while the venue allegedly used a broader or different interpretation.

## Kalshi's defense

The strongest available defense is implicit and comes from the counterarguments in the same Reddit thread.

One commenter wrote that there was a White House link and that the rules clearly counted repealing an executive order as issuing an executive order.[^reddit] A Kalshi-tagged/deleted account also stated that the outcome had already been explained on Kalshi Ideas and via support.[^reddit]

Because the contract language is not preserved in the reviewed evidence, the defense cannot be evaluated fully.

## Assessment

### Is there a specific identifiable dispute?

**Yes.** The Reddit thread identifies an executive-order count market and the alleged source mismatch.[^reddit]

### Is there enough evidence to establish that Kalshi violated the rules?

**No.** The exact contract text and official explanation are missing.

### Is there evidence on both sides?

**Yes.** The same thread contains the allegation and counterarguments that a White House action existed and that repeals counted.[^reddit]

### Was this a real failure for the tracker?

**Only as a blocker note.** It fits the general pattern of official-source/count ambiguity, but the present record is too thin to classify it as a real Kalshi mistake.

### Overall classification

- **Source/count dispute:** Alleged
- **Evidence quality:** Reddit-only
- **Platform rule violation:** Not established
- **Best public verdict:** Blocker pending exact contract and official explanation

## Precise blocker to a stronger finding

The missing evidence is the exact Kalshi contract terms, the archived market page, the WhiteHouse.gov page as it appeared at settlement, and Kalshi's full public or support explanation. Without those, the allegation cannot be resolved conclusively.
