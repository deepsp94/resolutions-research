# Kalshi: Bernie Sanders Greensboro rally mention market

## Bottom line

**Finding: The market exists, but the alleged resolution error is not verified. Confidence: low.**

The Grok seed claimed that a Kalshi market on what Bernie Sanders would say at a Greensboro rally resolved No despite video evidence that he used the exact words. It did not identify the disputed word or provide a supporting source.

The Kalshi page exists and shows three outcomes: `Trump`, `Billionaire`, and `Middle Class`, each resolved No.[^kalshi] But no reliable source was located showing that Sanders in fact said one of those exact terms under the market's matching rules, or that Kalshi later admitted an error.

## Market details

- **Venue:** Kalshi
- **Market:** `What will Bernie say at his Greensboro rally?`
- **Event:** Fighting Oligarchy Tour: Greensboro, NC.
- **Visible outcomes reviewed:** Trump, Billionaire, Middle Class.
- **Visible results:** No for each.
- **Rule:** the exact phrase/word, plural, or possessive had to be used; most grammatical/tense inflections were excluded; video was primary source, transcripts secondary.[^kalshi]

## What happened

The Kalshi page confirms the market existed and shows the relevant rules and No outcomes.[^kalshi]

The seed allegation says video evidence showed Sanders used the exact words, but the seed does not say which word, cite the video, quote the timestamp, or preserve a transcript. Searches during this pass did not locate a reliable external source making the same claim.

## The allegation against Kalshi

The allegation is that Kalshi resolved a mention market No even though the exact required term was said during the qualifying event.

If true, this would be a clear operational/source-review error. Mention markets should be mechanically verifiable from the video/transcript.

## Kalshi's defense

The strongest defense is the rule's exact-match standard.

The page says the exact word or phrase, plural, or possessive form counted, while other grammatical or tense inflections did not.[^kalshi] A user might think a related form or nearby phrase should count, while the contract excludes it.

Because no exact disputed word and timestamp are available, the defense cannot be tested.
