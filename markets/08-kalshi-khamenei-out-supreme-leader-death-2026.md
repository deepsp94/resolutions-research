# Kalshi: Ali Khamenei out as Supreme Leader

## Bottom line

**Finding: This was a real Kalshi presentation and rule-clarity failure. It is not a clean operational misgrade, because Kalshi's market page and leader-out terms support last-price treatment on death. Confidence: high on the customer-facing failure; medium on whether Kalshi violated the governing rule.**

Kalshi offered markets on whether Ali Khamenei would be out as Supreme Leader of Iran before several dates. After Khamenei was killed, traders who expected ordinary Yes payouts did not receive $1 per contract. Kalshi froze or paused the market and applied a death carveout, settling based on the last traded price before death or a fair value determined under its review process.[^kalshi-market][^washington-post][^verge]

The strongest allegation is that the visible market title said Khamenei would be "out as Supreme Leader," and death plainly made him out of office. Kalshi also heavily promoted the market, and some users saw winning indicators before Kalshi froze trading.[^washington-post]

Kalshi's strongest defense is that its rules expressly treated death differently: if the leader left solely because of death, the market would not resolve as a normal Yes, but would instead use last traded price or fair allocation. Kalshi said the rules were not changed after the fact, that U.S. regulatory constraints and its own policy prevent markets directly tied to death, and that it reimbursed losses or fees as a customer-service measure.[^kalshi-market][^wleaderout][^washington-post][^verge][^business-insider]

This belongs in the tracker as a **title/rules mismatch and death-carveout communication failure**. It should not be described simply as "Kalshi ignored its rules and paid the wrong side"; the harder question is whether the death carveout was adequately disclosed and stable during trading.

## The rule

Kalshi's public market page stated that if Ali Khamenei left office before the relevant date, the market would resolve Yes. It also included a death carveout: if Khamenei left solely because he died, Kalshi would determine payouts to long and short holders based on the last traded price before death.[^kalshi-market]

The `WLEADEROUT` contract terms contain the broader rule for world-leader-out markets. They define the payout criterion as the leader resigning or otherwise leaving the specified position. But they also state that if the natural person who is the primary subject dies before expiration, Kalshi may settle at the last traded price before death, or at a price before the circumstances causing death became known or reasonably anticipated.[^wleaderout]

The same terms permit the Outcome Review Committee to determine fair settlement value if no last traded price is fair.[^wleaderout]

That means the rule did not treat death as a normal, automatic $1 Yes event.

## What happened

Khamenei was killed in strikes on Iran, according to the reporting cited here.[^washington-post][^business-insider] Washington Post reported that a trader who had bet on Khamenei being out by March or April 1 saw green check marks in the Kalshi app indicating expected payouts, before Kalshi froze the market.[^washington-post]

Kalshi's market page states that, if Khamenei dies, the market resolves upon confirmed reporting of the death based on the last traded price before death; if that price is unclear, the Outcome Review Committee determines fair value. The page also notes that a prior clarification was grammatically ambiguous and that Kalshi would reimburse lost value for trades made between clarifications.[^kalshi-market]

The Verge reported that Kalshi CEO Tarek Mansour said positions would be paid at the last trading price before Khamenei's death, and that Kalshi was refunding fees and reimbursing people who bought shares after death.[^verge]

Business Insider reported that Kalshi paid about **$2.2 million** to resolve user complaints and later filed a CFTC rulebook amendment standardizing death treatment for markets where a natural person is central to the contract.[^business-insider][^cftc-amendment]

The Verge later reported that Kalshi faced a class action lawsuit alleging it failed to pay out as promised and that, according to the lawsuit, Kalshi did not implement the death carveout until after Khamenei's death.[^verge-lawsuit] That allegation remains unresolved in the sources reviewed here.

## The allegation against Kalshi

### 1. The title said "out," and death made him out

The market title communicated a broad ordinary-language question: whether Khamenei would be out as Supreme Leader. In ordinary terms, a leader who dies is out of office.

This is the core user-facing mismatch. A user could read the title and reasonably expect a normal Yes if Khamenei was no longer Supreme Leader by the deadline, regardless of mechanism.

### 2. The carveout was hard to reconcile with the trading product

Kalshi's defense was that the market was not a death market because Khamenei could leave power by resignation or transition. But for an aging autocratic leader in wartime, many traders treated death as one of the most salient paths to "out." Washington Post quoted critics arguing that Kalshi could have titled the market more narrowly if it meant to exclude death as a normal Yes path.[^washington-post]

That does not prove bad faith. It does show that the visible product and detailed settlement logic diverged in a way likely to confuse ordinary users.

### 3. The clarification history damaged trust

Kalshi's own page says a prior clarification was grammatically ambiguous and offers reimbursement for lost value on trades made between clarifications.[^kalshi-market] Washington Post reported that Kalshi acknowledged a grammatically ambiguous post and offered reimbursements, while maintaining it had not changed the rules after the incident.[^washington-post]

That is material. Even if the death carveout existed, Kalshi's own conduct indicates that some user-facing explanation was not clear enough.

### 4. The app apparently showed users they had won

Washington Post reported that at least one trader's app displayed green check marks and expected payouts after Khamenei's death, before Kalshi froze the trade.[^washington-post]

That is not proof of final settlement error, but it is evidence of a product communication failure at the exact moment when traders were trying to understand whether the event had resolved.

## Kalshi's defense

Kalshi's defense has four parts.

First, the written market rules included a death carveout. The Kalshi page and `WLEADEROUT` terms both state that death of the primary subject can be settled by last traded price or fair allocation rather than a normal Yes payout.[^kalshi-market][^wleaderout]

Second, Kalshi argued that it does not list markets directly tied to death and designs rules to prevent people from profiting from death. The Verge and Washington Post both report this as the company's stated rationale.[^verge][^washington-post]

Third, Kalshi pointed to regulatory constraints. The Washington Post reported that the CFTC bans bets involving or referencing terrorism, assassination, or war, and Business Insider reported that Kalshi filed a later CFTC amendment to clarify how death affects markets where a natural person is central.[^washington-post][^business-insider][^cftc-amendment]

Fourth, Kalshi attempted customer remediation. It reimbursed fees, post-death purchases, or lost value in at least some categories, and Business Insider reported the company paid about $2.2 million to resolve complaints.[^verge][^business-insider]

## Assessment

### Did Khamenei leaving office by death satisfy the ordinary market title?

**Yes.**

The ordinary-language reading of "out as Supreme Leader" includes death. That is why the controversy was predictable.

### Did Kalshi's written rules make death a normal $1 Yes event?

**No, on the available public record.**

The market page and `WLEADEROUT` terms both support last-price or fair-value settlement when the relevant person dies.[^kalshi-market][^wleaderout]

### Did Kalshi make a platform mistake?

**Yes.**

The mistake was not necessarily the final settlement formula. The established mistake was offering and promoting a high-volume "out as Supreme Leader" market while relying on a death carveout that was not prominent or clear enough for many traders, then having to clarify, pause, reimburse, and later standardize the rule.

### Did Kalshi violate the contract?

**Not proven from the sources reviewed.**

There is a lawsuit allegation that Kalshi implemented the death carveout only after Khamenei died.[^verge-lawsuit] But the current market page and contract terms support Kalshi's rule-based defense, and the reporting reviewed here does not conclusively reconstruct the exact rule text as displayed at every point before death.

### Was this a real failure for the tracker?

**Yes.**

This is a major failure in market presentation and rule communication. It produced public backlash, reimbursement, a CFTC rule amendment, and litigation. It is exactly the kind of case where a short market title and dense settlement language caused materially different user expectations.

### Overall classification

- **Operational settlement error:** Not established
- **Rule-following defense:** Strong, based on current page and `WLEADEROUT` terms
- **Title/rules mismatch:** Yes
- **Clarification failure:** Yes
- **Customer remediation:** Yes, including reported $2.2 million complaint-resolution cost
- **Conclusive verdict:** Platform communication/design failure; contract violation unresolved

## Precise blocker to a stronger finding

The missing evidence is a timestamped archive of the exact market page, clarification text, and attached `WLEADEROUT` terms as they appeared before Khamenei's death and before Kalshi's later clarification. Without that archive, it is not possible to conclusively decide the lawsuit-style allegation that the death carveout was added only after the event.
